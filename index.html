About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Stanford University ASL group, working with Prof. Marco Pavone. I obtained my Ph.D. at Cornell University, advised by Prof. Kilian Q. Weinberger and Prof. Bharath Hariharan. My research interest lies in visual understanding of the world, including 3D perception and multi-modal learning, combining visual data with other sensory inputs to enhance environmental understanding.

I am fortunate to have been supported by a Cornell University Fellowship, an Nvidia Graduate Student Fellowship, and an American Association of University Women (AAUW) Dissertation Fellowship Award. Before joining Cornell University, I had the pleasure of working on a variety of topics including applied machine learning, deep reinforcement learning, and applications of natural language processing. Invited Talks

Leveraging Auxiliary Information for Self-Driving Perception across Diverse End Users
Colloquium talk for the Cornell University CS Ph.D. Students Colloquium. This talk discusses novel approaches to autonomous vehicle perception that reduce reliance on expensive 3D labeled datasets by leveraging auxiliary information sources like repeated traversal data and navigation maps.
Dec 5, 2024 Cornell University, New York
Unsupervised Objects Discovery from LiDAR Scans for Autonomous Driving
Seminar talk for the researchers from the Australian Centre for Robotics (ACFR) at the University of Sydney. This talk covered how we can shift away from supervised training methods for 3D detection in self-driving, and instead train 3D object detectors without any labels at all.
May 2, 2024 Australian Centre for Robotics, University of Sydney, Australia
Leveraging Auxiliary Information for Self-Driving Perception
The fast-forward talk following the Nvidia 2023 Graduate Student Fellowship year at the Nvidia GTC 2024. Discussed incorporating auxiliary information such as structure and standard definition maps to improve self-driving perception.
Mar 19, 2024 Nvidia GTC, San Jose Convention Center
The Under-Utilized Information in Repeated Traversals for Autonomous Vehicles
The inaugural talk for the P1 Cafe, Pioneer Center. Introducing works from the self-driving research group at Cornell University.
